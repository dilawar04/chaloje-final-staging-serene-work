CPEC

China-Pakistan Economic Corridor


In April of 2015, Pakistan and China announced their intention to develop the multi billion dollar China–Pakistan Economic Corridor(CPEC), which in turn forms part of China's ambitious 3 Trillion Dollar ‘One Belt, One Road’ Initiative.

CPEC is a 3,218 kilometer long route, to be built over next several years, consisting of highways, railways and pipelines. The actual estimated cost of the project is expected to be US$75 billion, out of which in access of US$45 billion was spent to ensure that the corridor becomes operational by 2020. The remaining investment is being spent on energy generation and infrastructure development.
CPEC will not only benefit China and Pakistan but will have a positive impact on Iran, Afghanistan, Central Asian Republic, and the region as whole. The enhancement of geographical linkages having state-of-the-art road, rail and air transportation system with frequent and free exchanges of growth and people to people contact will result in a well connected and integrated region of shared destiny, harmony and development. *Some excerpts were taken from the official CPEC website: http://cpec.gov.pk/index

The New Gwadar Master Plan

Pakistan’s first ‘tax free’ & ‘weapons free’ city

Prepared by Chinese state-owned company China Communications Construction Company in conjunction with Pakistan's Minister of Planning, Development & Reform and Gwadar Development Authority, the New Gwadar Master Plan chalks out an elaborate road map on how Gwadar is to become the trade and economic hub of South Asia with a GDP per capita of $15,000. [10 times that of Pakistan's average.]

According to the new master plan, the city will become the fulcrum of economic development in western Pakistan, the main port in western Pakistan, one of the westward sea routes in western China, the five Central Asian countries and the trade centers of Afghanistan, South Asia and the neighboring Middle East.

Gwadar is set to see a massive influx of skilled workers and high powered executives as it gears up to become the technological, industrial and high-tech service hub of South Asia as Gwadar's economic output is expected to exceed $30 Billion whilst creating up to 1.2 Million jobs.

In light of this massive influx, Gwadar will require some 15,800 new homes by 2025, 47,600 by 2030 and 254,500 by 2050.

The current supply of quality housing in Gwadar's is in the 100's at most and rental demand is burgeoning putting upward pressure on prices. With a ban on new NOC's and limited supply within the private sector coming through we can expect to see a massive boom in property prices in Gwadar in the coming years.

Pakistan's first 'tax-free' & 'weapon-free' haven Gwadar's development will see the crescent-shaped man-made islands representing Pakistan's flag, grand theaters, concert halls, cultural exchange centres, a university city, lakeside shopping mall, waterfront shopping and leisure promenades, parks, golden beaches, 5* hotels and cruise ships linking to Muscat, Dubai, Doha, Bahrain and Jeddah to name a few.
